METHODOLOGY

Tailored Transition
Pathways.

Career transitions are rarely linear. A senior leader exploring a move to a smaller company may also be weighing a board advisory portfolio. A technologist considering public sector impact may simultaneously be evaluating fractional consulting. The reality is that most professionals in transition are navigating two or three of these pathways at once — testing assumptions, narrowing options, and looking for the signal that tells them where they belong next.

That complexity is not a problem to solve. It is the problem we were built for.

Our advisory is structured around seven distinct transition archetypes. Each represents a pattern we see repeatedly in the senior professionals we work with. Your Market Scan, strategy calls, and execution plan are shaped by whichever pathways apply to you — because the best career intelligence doesn't force you into a single lane. It maps the territory and helps you choose.

01

Same Job Type

For professionals who know exactly what they do and want to keep doing it — but in a stronger position. You are not pivoting. You are competing in a market that has changed around you, and you need updated positioning, AI fluency, and a search strategy built for how hiring actually works today. This is about sharpening the blade, not changing direction.

Often driven by: A desire for better compensation, escaping a toxic culture, or an unexpected layoff where the goal is a rapid, strategic return to familiar territory.

02

Strategic Career Pivot

Translating domain knowledge into a compelling narrative for high-growth adjacent roles within the same sector. We help you map your existing expertise to new titles, bridge the gap in perception, and position your lateral move as a deliberate, high-value transition.

Often driven by: Hitting a growth ceiling in your current function, or recognizing that an adjacent role offers stronger long-term equity and leverage.

03

Full Career Reinvention

Architecting a total professional identity shift, mapping skills directly into an entirely new target sector. We rebuild your professional narrative from the ground up, proving your value to decision-makers who don't yet speak your language.

Often driven by: Industry burnout, a profound shift in personal priorities, or the realization that your legacy sector is permanently contracting.

04

Contract & Advisory

Shifting away from full-time employment into a portfolio of fractional or advisory roles and Board seats. We help you package your accumulated expertise into discrete, marketable advisory offerings and target the founders and boards who need them.

Often driven by: A desire for autonomy, a recent liquidity event, or a conscious uncoupling from the 60-hour corporate grind.

05

Entrepreneurial Launchpad

For leaders ready to exit corporate life and position their expertise to successfully launch their own venture. We provide the market intelligence and strategic framing to help you transition from employee to founder with confidence and clarity.

Often driven by: Spotting an unserved market need during your corporate tenure, or finally securing the financial runway to build on your own terms.

06

Leadership Ascent

Crafting the strategic narrative and executive presence required to secure a step-up into the C-Suite. This pathway focuses heavily on stakeholder mapping, executive-level interview preparation, and demonstrating readiness for enterprise-wide impact.

Often driven by: Being passed over internally, or recognizing that your current organization cannot accommodate your ambition for the top table.

07

Public & Mission-Driven Transition

For experienced professionals drawn toward roles where impact is measured in policy, public outcomes, or societal change rather than revenue. This pathway covers moves into government, semi-state bodies, regulatory agencies, NGOs, international organisations, healthcare, education, and mission-driven enterprises.

Whether navigating PAS/publicjobs.ie in Ireland, Civil Service Jobs in the UK, or USAJobs, we help translate commercial leadership experience into the specific language, networks, and rigorous application processes these jurisdictions require — because the skills transfer, but the playbook does not.

Often driven by: A fundamental desire for legacy-building impact and public service over commercial scale and revenue targets.
